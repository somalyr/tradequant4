Spot Above Vol Trigger — Moves Dampened
Dealers are long gamma. They buy dips and sell rips automatically — acting as a shock absorber. Moves get dampened. 0DTE premium decays faster. Harder to hold calls. Best suited for premium selling or very tight scalps.
Deep Negative GEX Across All Strikes — Acceleration

Max Pain Below Spot — Tailwind for Puts
No closing-hour headwind. Market makers are not structurally fighting your puts. You have more freedom to hold through the afternoon session if the trend is intact.
Max Pain At Spot — Pin Risk
Price gets sticky. Market makers are already near their profit maximum. Avoid directional 0DTE. This is a premium sell day — sell puts below and calls above instead of buying direction.
📈 VIX RULES▼
VIX Suppressed — Maximum Edge for Puts
When VIX is below its 5-day average, puts are underpriced relative to actual risk. When the move comes, you get double the return — delta AND vega expansion simultaneously. This is the best time to buy 0DTE puts. Size up to full contracts.
VIX Elevated — Puts Expensive, Size Down
Fear is already priced in. You are buying expensive premium. The market has to move further just to break even. Size down 30-50% or skip. The edge is gone when IV is high.
VIX Spiking Intraday — Do Not Chase
An intraday VIX spike inflates put premiums instantly. The best entries are already gone. Wait for VIX to settle before entering. Entries on a spike are almost always traps — the initial panic flush is the move, and you missed it.
🔮 MOJO RULES▼
Goblue RED — Bears Only
Market internals are bearish. Only take put trades today regardless of what GEX shows. Do not buy calls. If GEX is bullish but Goblue is RED — wait for 10:30 rule to confirm before doing anything.
Goblue GREEN — Bulls Only
Market internals are bullish. Only take call trades today. Do not buy puts. If GEX is bearish but Goblue is GREEN — wait for 10:30 rule before entering either direction.
Mojo Conflicts With GEX — Wait for 10:30
When GEX says one direction and Goblue says the other, both signals cancel out. Do not trade until the 10:30am rule confirms direction. Above 10:30 high = follow bull signal. Below 10:30 low = follow bear signal.
GoblueVIX RED — Bearish Edge Confirmed
When GoblueVIX is red alongside Goblue red, the volatility structure is also bearish. This is the highest conviction bear signal from Mojo. Adds confidence to put trades.
📊 DEX + FLOW RULES▼

Highest conviction bear setup. Delta positioning is bearish AND real money is buying puts. Both structural and flow signals align. This is the setup you want for maximum size (within NYMO rules).

DEX Battle + Call Buying — Range Mode
Negative delta is fighting call buying. Neither side can break structure. Price is contained. Do not buy directional options. Sell put spreads below GEX floor and call spreads above call wall. Collect premium and let the battle keep price pinned.

DEX and Flow Conflict — Wait for Ribbon
When delta positioning says one direction but volume flow says another, smart money is split. Do not enter until the 3m ribbon confirms direction. The ribbon is the tiebreaker.
⚡ GENERAL 0DTE RULES▼
Always Take 55% at T1
You will be tempted to hold for the full move. Do not. Take 55% off at T1 every single time. Move stop to breakeven on the runner. The times you hold through T1 and it reverses will cost you more than the times you miss the full move to T3.
Stop is a 3m Candle Close — Not a Touch
Price touches your stop level constantly in volatile sessions. A touch is noise. A 3m candle CLOSE past your stop level is the signal. Only exit on a close, not a wick. This prevents getting shaken out of valid trades by normal intraday volatility.
Size Kills More Traders Than Direction
Being right on direction but too large means getting stopped out on the morning shakeout. Half the contracts with a correct read beats full contracts on a correct read that stops you out before the move. Size is risk management, not conviction expression.
One Trade Per Session
The 10am flush OR the 2:30pm breakdown. Not both. The second trade of the day is almost always revenge trading or FOMO. If you missed the 10am entry, your job is to wait for 2:30pm or go flat. Forcing a second entry rarely works.
Never Chase the Break — Wait for the Retest
When a level breaks, the instinct is to enter immediately. Don't. Wait for price to bounce back to the broken level, fail to reclaim it, and then enter on the 3m close confirmation. The R:R on a retest is 3-5x better than chasing the initial break.
